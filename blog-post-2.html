5 Tips for Better Password Management

Date: 2023-10-27

  1. Use a Password Manager: A password manager is a secure vault that stores all your passwords. You only need to remember one master password to access them. It simplifies the process of using unique, complex passwords for every site.
  2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): 2FA adds an extra layer of security. Even if a hacker steals your password, they will still need a second factor (like a code from your phone) to log in.
  3. Avoid Personal Information: Do not use easily guessable information in your passwords, such as your name, birthday, or pet's name. This information is often publicly available and can be used to guess your password.
  4. Conduct Regular Password Audits: Periodically review your passwords. Change any that are weak or have been involved in a known data breach. Many password managers have a built-in audit feature.
  5. Use a Strong, Unique Password for Your Email: Your primary email account is often the key to resetting passwords for your other accounts. Protect it with your strongest and most unique password.
