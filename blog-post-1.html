The Dangers of Weak Passwords

Date: 2023-10-27

Weak passwords are a hacker's best friend. Passwords that are short, common, or easily guessable (like "123456" or "password") can be cracked in seconds using automated tools. Once a hacker gains access to one of your accounts, they can potentially access your personal information, financial data, and even lock you out of your own accounts.

Using the same weak password across multiple sites is even more dangerous. If one site experiences a data breach, hackers will try that same username and password combination on other popular sites, a technique known as "credential stuffing." This can lead to a domino effect, compromising your entire online presence.

To protect yourself, it is crucial to use a unique and strong password for every single online account. A strong password should be long (at least 12 characters), and include a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
